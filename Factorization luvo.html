What is Factorization?

Factorization is the process of breaking down a number or expression into its simplest parts, called factors. In algebra, we often factorize quadratic expressions to make them easier to solve.

For example: To factorize \(x^2 + 5x + 6\), we look for two numbers that multiply to 6 and add up to 5. The factors are (x + 2)(x + 3).

Examples of Factorization

Example 1:

Factorize \(x^2 + 7x + 10\)

Solution: We need two numbers that multiply to 10 and add up to 7. The factors are (x + 5)(x + 2).

Example 2:

Factorize \(x^2 - 4\)

Solution: This is a difference of squares, so it can be factorized as (x + 2)(x - 2).

Practice Exercises

Try to factorize the following expressions:

  • 1. \(x^2 + 6x + 9\)
  • 2. \(x^2 - 9\)
  • 3. \(x^2 + 8x + 15\)

Answers:

  • 1. (x + 3)(x + 3)
  • 2. (x + 3)(x - 3)
  • 3. (x + 3)(x + 5)
